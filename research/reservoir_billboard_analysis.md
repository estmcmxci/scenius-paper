# Reservoir / Billboard "On The Record" — Catalog Market Analysis

**Source:** Billboard "On The Record" podcast — "Why Music Catalogs from Taylor Swift, Bob Dylan & Miles Davis Are Hot Investments"
**Guest:** Golnar Khosrowshahi, founder & CEO of Reservoir Media (est. 2007)
**Host:** Kristen Robinson
**Date:** 2026 (exact air date TBD)

---

## Summary

19-year veteran catalog investor describes the market structure, valuation methodology, deal flow mechanics, and emerging risks in the music catalog investment space. Interview covers the full arc from 2007 (2-3x multiples, piracy fear, streaming uncertainty) to 2026 (15-20x multiples, institutional demand, $1B+ annual deal flow).

---

## Key Claims from the Interview

### 1. Valuation Multiples Have 10x'd in 19 Years

Catalogs traded at 2-4x gross profit in 2007. Today: 15-20x, with some higher. The inflection came when streaming growth outpaced the "digital deflation" of the piracy era. Institutional investors (KKR, Dutch pension funds) were present early — Hipgnosis drove retail awareness and price escalation.

### 2. Revenue Decay Rate Is the Central Valuation Question

When asked why genres are valued differently, Khosrowshahi reduces it to: "How widespread is the listenership? And how long is that going to last? At what rate is the revenue on this music going to decay?" Songs with cultural permanence (e.g. "Take Me Home, Country Roads") command premiums. Songs that are "hits in the moment" but lack staying power trade lower regardless of peak performance.

### 3. Deal Flow Is Unstructured

Described as "the most unregulated, disenfranchised deal flow structure of a business of this size." No portal. No standard mechanism. Deals come through lawyers, managers, family members, word of mouth. Larger deals go through formal representatives; smaller ones are relationship-driven. Reservoir executes most significant deals off-market.

### 4. Re-Emergence Is Valuable but Unprojectable

Multiple re-emergence vectors discussed:
- **Biopics:** Johnny Cash biopic, Bob Dylan (Timothy Chalamet), upcoming Love Story
- **TV syncs:** Grey's Anatomy pioneered music discovery via TV. Euphoria S2 featured Reservoir's Sinead O'Connor catalog. Yellowstone drove country catalog upticks
- **Sample flipping:** De La Soul catalog cleared after 18-month sample clearance; Bridget Mendler interpolated the Delfonics' "Ready or Not"
- **Cultural activations:** Miles Davis centennial — fashion collabs, Lexus commercial, social media campaigns driving streaming growth

Khosrowshahi acknowledges these are hard to project but essential to value creation. The interviewer asks directly: "How do you account for those random blips on the radar that are really positive for a catalog?" Answer: due diligence on sync potential, genre fit, and whether the catalog has been "neglected" (untapped licensing opportunity).

### 5. Financial vs. Music-Company Buyers

Two distinct buyer classes with different time horizons and priorities:
- **Financial investors** (KKR, Shamrock, pension funds): 3-5 year exit, mid-teens unlevered IRR target. Don't care about artist reputation risk. Increasingly recognizing that 10-15 year holds outperform 5-year exits for these assets.
- **Music companies** (Reservoir, majors): 10-30 year horizon. Active platform with creative services, administration, marketing. Care about artist reputation and legacy stewardship. Pass on deals with character risk.

This spread creates different pricing for the same asset depending on buyer class.

### 6. Second Sales Are Accelerating

Financial investors are flipping catalogs on 3-5 year cycles. "We're reading about it every day." Each resale creates a new valuation event.

### 7. Masters vs. Publishing Are Distinct Asset Classes

Now trade at parity, but require different teams, marketing, and operational infrastructure:
- **Masters:** Active marketing required (vinyl, promotion, distribution)
- **Publishing:** More passive — "checks come in the mail"

Publishing buyers must consider who owns the masters and whether they're being actively marketed.

### 8. AI Impact Is Currently Neutral

Reservoir projects neither upside (AI platform licensing income) nor downside (cannibalization) into valuations. Exception: commodity/production music is being displaced. Core artist-driven catalogs are unaffected for now.

### 9. People Still Seek Tastemakers

Interviewer: "People are still seeking gatekeepers... whether it's a TV show with a great soundtrack or an influencer online... people are always looking for someone that they can trust." Grey's Anatomy was a "goal for songwriters" to get synced on. Euphoria fills the same role for a younger generation.

### 10. Attention Span and Music Oversupply

Khosrowshahi worries about cultural expiration: "At what point is a catalog that is 30, 40, 50 years old just no longer relevant?" Shorter attention spans + more music choice + more listening hours may dilute rather than concentrate catalog value over time.

## Relevance to Scenius

### Validates

| Research Claim | Interview Evidence |
|---|---|
| Young catalogs are underpriced (Duetti MFI 3.2x gap) | 2-4x → 15-20x trajectory confirms massive repricing opportunity for early identification |
| Backward-looking DCF models miss cultural inflection points | Khosrowshahi confirms valuation is historical cash flow + sync judgment; re-emergence is "random blips" that can't be modeled |
| Institutional data infrastructure is thin for emerging catalogs | Deal flow is unstructured; no portal, no standard mechanism; relationship-driven |
| Demand for tastemaker curation persists (Mulligan thesis) | "People are still seeking gatekeepers... someone they can trust" |
| Sub-$1M catalog segment is the growth area | Reservoir sees $1B+ deal flow but worries about finite top-tier supply; smaller catalogs are the expanding frontier |

### New Inputs

- **Decay rate as the core question:** Scenius's breakout prediction could be reframed as a "cultural decay rate forecast" — not just "will this artist break out?" but "how long will this music remain culturally relevant?"
- **Second sales as recurring valuation events:** Each flip creates fresh demand for pricing signals. Growing market for Scenius's output.
- **Masters vs. publishing distinction:** Prediction market design may need to distinguish between these asset classes. A "breakout event" for publishing (sync placement) differs from masters (streaming growth).
- **Off-market deal structure:** Reputation-weighted conviction signals could become a discovery tool for buyers who currently rely on word-of-mouth deal flow.
- **Character risk as valuation factor:** Music companies discount catalogs with reputation risk; financial investors don't. This qualitative judgment is exactly the kind of signal tastemakers could formalize.

### Does Not Address

- The mechanism design question (capital-weighted vs. reputation-weighted aggregation)
- SoundCloud as signal source
- Onchain attestation or DeFi infrastructure
- Emerging/independent artist catalogs specifically (Reservoir operates at institutional scale)
