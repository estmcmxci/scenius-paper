# Scenius Product Positioning: Blog + SoundCloud + Reputation Market

**Date:** February 20, 2026

---

## The Three-Layer Architecture

### SoundCloud = Infrastructure

SoundCloud is the venue where cultural discovery already happens. It has the early listening data, curator graphs, repost networks, and pre-breakout audience formation. Scenius is an application built on SoundCloud — consuming its data as an API, weighting it by demonstrated accuracy, and outputting reputation-weighted breakout probabilities that catalog buyers can act on.

SoundCloud doesn't need to change anything. Scenius makes their data more valuable the same way Coinbase calls Morpho for lending rates or Polymarket built on crypto rails — Walden's "markets as APIs" pattern.

### scenius.blog = Surface

The blog is the narrative and media layer. It's where readers come to discover artists through long-form writing, profiles, and essays. This directly addresses Mulligan's diagnosis: curation has become "frictionless, invisible and automated," and there are no new music journalists because the platform layer killed the economic model for narrative-driven discovery.

scenius.blog brings journalism back by changing the economic model underneath it. Writers don't get paid for attention — they get paid for accuracy.

### Reputation-Weighted Prediction Market = Mechanism

The prediction market runs underneath the blog surface. Writers and readers stake reputation on which artists will break out. Accuracy compounds into a reputation score that becomes a legible, composable signal for catalog pricing.

---

## How the Layers Reinforce Each Other

| Layer | What users see | What the mechanism sees |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Blog** | Essays, artist profiles, discovery | Narrative as prediction signal |
| **Predictions** | "I think this artist breaks out" | Reputation-weighted market participation |
| **Reputation** | Leaderboards, track record | Calibrated accuracy scores feeding catalog repricing |

The writing *is* the market participation. The narrative *is* the prediction.

---

## The User Funnel

1. **Read** — Discover artists through narrative (the journalism layer Mulligan says is dead)
2. **Predict** — Stake reputation on who breaks out (the market layer Walden describes)
3. **Earn** — Compound reputation from accuracy (the mechanism Scenius provides)

Media is the distribution. The market is the backend. Reputation is the product.

---

## Why This Works: Solving Three Problems Simultaneously

### Mulligan's Problem — No New Music Journalists

Traditional music journalism died because attention couldn't be monetized. Scenius changes the economic model: your attention isn't the product — your *accuracy* is. Write about an artist before they break out, be right consistently, and your reputation score compounds. You become a legible signal that catalog buyers pay attention to.

### Walden's Problem — Markets Need Cultural Signal

Walden argues "invest in what you know" is increasingly possible through permissionless markets, but doesn't address how markets should aggregate signal from heterogeneous participants. Scenius provides the micro-mechanism correction: reputation-weighting ensures that informed-but-capital-constrained tastemakers (the writers, curators, early fans) aren't drowned out by wealthy-but-uninformed participants.

### The Catalog Pricing Problem — Young Catalogs Are Mispriced

Young music catalogs (2–5 years old) trade at a uniform ~3.2x revenue multiple. Mature catalogs trade at 5.8–8.7x. The gap exists because backward-looking data can't distinguish future breakouts from one-hit wonders. Scenius's reputation-weighted blog produces forward-looking conviction signals that compress the uncertainty discount for specific catalogs — repricing from 3.2x to 5x when the signal is strong.

## The SoundCloud Partnership Pitch

We're building an application on your platform that makes your data more valuable. SoundCloud gets a new product surface that monetizes long-tail catalog data. Scenius gets the signal environment where tastemaker behavior is already happening. Users see a blog. The mechanism sees a prediction market. Catalog buyers see actionable price signals.
